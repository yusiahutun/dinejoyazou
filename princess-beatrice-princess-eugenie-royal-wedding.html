Princesses Eugenie and Beatrice Toned It Down With Their Hats at this Royal Wedding

Princesses Beatrice and Eugenie's outfits for the last royal wedding inspired countless memes—especially that bow-meets-pretzel hat. So it comes as little surprise that the sisters have played it safer this time around.

Eugenie, 28, who will herself be the next bride to walk down the aisle at St George's Chapel when she marries Jack Brooksbank in October, wore a pale blue 60s-inspired shift dress by Gainsbourg. She teamed it with a pillbox hat by milliner Fiona Graham, Valentino Rockstud heels and carried a buff-colored bag by M2Malletier. She and her fiancé are neighbours of the new Duke and Duchess of Sussex and the two women are believed to have become close friends.

Princess Beatrice in a Roksanda Viola dress and a Stephen Jones headpiece.

Beatrice, 29, wore an embellished midi-length teal dress by Roksanda Viola. Her headpiece was significantly more understated than the one she chose in 2011: a headband by Stephen Jones.

Back in 2011, both Beatrice and Eugenie wore hats by famed milliner Philip Treacy. And while they may not have won any style points, Beatrice went on to sell her headpiece for charity, raising an impressive £81,000.

Beatrice, who split from long-term boyfriend Dave Clark last summer, arrived for Harry and Meghan's wedding with her father, Prince Andrew. Today, it emerged that Dave is now engaged to his American girlfriend, Lynn Anderson. Beatrice is believed to have already congratulated the couple, and may even attend their wedding.

Getty Images

Princesses Eugenie (left) and Beatrice (right) at the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge’s wedding in 2011.

Beatrice and Eugenie's mother, Sarah Ferguson, the Duchess of York, who divorced their father in 1996, was also in attendance but arrived solo and was seated separately from the royals. It is understood that the Duchess was invited to the ceremony, but not to the evening reception.

Sarah, 58, looked elegant in a navy fitted dress and matching jacket with a pale pink collar. She accessorized with a navy fascinator, and curiously, only one glove.
