Self-tan tips for your summer holiday

Harper's Bazaar

We know sunbathing isn't healthy, so if you want a beautiful tan this summer, fake it. James Harknett, the global creative consultant at Fake Bake, lays down the golden rules for self-tanning before a holiday – and the best ways to keep your colour after.

Why should I fake tan before a holiday?

"Having a spray tan for when you first arrive at your destination stops that overwhelming desire to lay out all day and potentially damage and burn the skin. I'd recommend using Fake Bake's Amplify, £18, a week before travelling. This multipurpose product not only starts to build a golden glow but speeds up the melanin production on the skin, making it easier to tan naturally. But, of course, it's not a sunscreen substitute so never forget to apply your SPF."

Do I need to avoid the swimming pool?

"Try and avoid going in the pool on the first few days after getting a spray tan, as the chlorine can strip your fake-tan colour prematurely. Also avoid using tanning oil and use a high SPF that can be spritzed onto the body instead."

With all the suncream, do I still need to moisturise?

"Yes. Moisturise twice a day with something oil-free and lightweight. Vaseline's Intensive Care Aloe Soothe Spray Moisturiser, £3.29, is ideal."

My tan always goes patchy, how can I avoid it?

"Sprinkle a little Johnson's Baby Powder, £1.29, in your bikini top and in the joints of the body that are prone to perspiring in a hot climate. This will help stop the colour rubbing away in the heat. The same is true when you get home. In particular, sprinkle a little talc in your sports bra and trainers if you are exercising."

Should I top up my fake tan on holiday?

"You can keep your glow intact by applying Fake Bake Fair, £24.95, to boost your tan. It's a gradual tanner with a warm butterscotch guide colour to allow you to avoid streaking while achieving a natural glow."

How else can I make my tan last longer?

"The first thing to do to keep your holiday skin glowing longer is keep it hydrated. Keeping the skin supple and the tan nourished will extend its colour. A couple of days after returning from holiday, give the skin a gentle exfoliation to even out the tone. Avoid having any baths when you return with a tan as the hot water can soak away the colour prematurely. Keep showers cooler than normal and pat dry the skin."

Eek! I've got tan lines, what should I do?

"If you're left with random white bits, a thin layer of self-tan will help even up the skin tone, but it's good to remember that self-tan works with our dead skin cells and proteins on the surface, so it will also darken the existing natural tan. It will leave you feeling more body confident with the white bits more blended and golden, though."
