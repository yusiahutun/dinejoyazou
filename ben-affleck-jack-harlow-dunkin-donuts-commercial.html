Ben Affleck Ignores Advice From Rapper Jack Harlow in Latest Dunkin' Commercial

He may have two Oscars to his name and a superstar wife by his side, but Ben Affleck will always run on Dunkin’.

The iced coffee fanatic has been the star of a number of commercials for the doughnut-and-coffee brand recently, the latest of which features “First Class” rapper and White Men Can’t Jump actor Jack Harlow.

The new spot serves as a sequel to the Dunkin’ ad that appeared during the Grammys last Sunday, in which Affleck calls up wife J.Lo to share the dream he’s just had about being a pop star, and to discuss the possibility of appearing on her new album.

In the new commercial, the Argo director and star sits in a car with Harlow, explaining why he’s destined for a career in music. “Here’s why I know it’s right,” Affleck begins. “It’s ’cause there’s a voice [telling me]: ‘Don’t do this terrible mistake.’”

Harlow—sounding like Affleck’s bro therapist—says, “That might be what you need to hear.” To which Affleck fires back, “No. That’s the wrong voice.”

As a comical closer, Harlow makes a last-ditch effort, asking, “What if it’s a voice of reason?” Affleck replies, “Nah, I don’t have that voice, believe me.”

The Massachusetts native and Gone Girl actor has become an official ambassador for the Boston-born doughnut-and-coffee chain—which makes sense, given that he’s been spotted numerous times on the street holding his trusty Dunkin’ iced coffee and even wearing the company’s merch.

At the end of the commercial, a message pops up: “To Be Continued.” So we can rest assured this isn’t the last we’ll see of Ben and Dunkin’.
