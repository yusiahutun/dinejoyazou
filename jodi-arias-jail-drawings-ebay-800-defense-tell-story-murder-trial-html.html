Jodi Arias jail drawings on eBay for over $800 as defense tell their side of the story in murder tri

Drawings that Jodi Arias made in JAIL posted on eBay and on sale for over $800 as defense begins to tell their side of the story in murder trial

By Meghan Keneally

Published: 09:27 EDT, 22 January 2013 | Updated: 09:36 EDT, 22 January 2013

Jodi Arias is now selling off drawings that she made while waiting in jail for her court date over the murder of her ex-boyfriend Travis Alexander.

The two drawings are pictures of famed movie star Grace Kelly and one of an unidentified model that Arias titled 'Pisces #2' and their prices are on the rise.

The Grace Kelly portrait is currently fetching $305 but the Pisces piece is on the rise with a price tag of $810.

Friend on the outside: An unidentified person is selling works of art that Jodi Arias reportedly made while in prison

On the auction block: With ten hours left of bidding, the Grace Kelly portrait (left) was going for $305 and the picture of a model titled Pisces #2 (right) was fetching $810

Bidding will end early Tuesday evening, the same day that Arias' defense attorneys will begin their portion of the trial, arguing that she killed her former lover as an act of self defense.

According to the eBay posting, the drawings were made by Arias in 2000 when she was incarcerated for the 1998 slaying.

She does not appear to be the person actually selling the drawings on the site, however, as it reads: 'Jodi thanks everyone that has helped out during these tough times.  Please continue to pray for her family and for Travis' family.'

ABC News reports that the seller's name is not listed, only giving the username 0817soldierofchrist.

It does seem like a distinct possibility that they have a true connection to the suspected felon as they are based out of Redding, California, which is where Arias rented the car that she drove from her hometown to Mesa, Arizona on the night that she killed Alexander.

The only other items the seller has posted to the auction site are postcards featuring the controversial Arizona sheriff Joe Arpaio, who has made a name for himself among conservatives for being tough on immigration policy and enacting chain gangs.

Conflicting stories: Before she admitted to killing Travis Alexander, she denied it on the basis on the way he was killed as too gruesome for her

Even though the sale of these two pieces ends on Tuesday night, it will not be the last appearance of Arias' artwork.

The seller says on the eBay post that the Pisces drawing is a part of a zodiac series, and her Hollywood infatuation will be continued with upcoming drawings of Frank Sinatra and Lucille Ball.

It is little surprise that Arias, a professional photographer, turned to art while se was confined in jail for years awaiting trial. That said, the prices for the works may have some balking in astonishment.

Tuesday marks the beginning of the defense portion of the trial, and in the final days of the prosecution, lawyers brought more personal items that belonged into the alleged murderer into question.

Doomed: Jodi Arias pictured with Travis Alexander, the ex-boyfriend that she is accused of stabbing 27 times after her tried to end their relationship

New insight: Some recent evidence included excerpts from her diary

Excerpts from Arias' diary were read to the court and show that in the days following the Alexander's murder- which she eventually admitted to but claimed was done in self defense- she lied not only to detectives but to her own diary as well.

In an entry dated just days after she stabbed Alexander 27 times, slit his throat and shot him in the face, she wrote: 'Travis is dead. What happened?!? Travis, what is this...

Three days after his body was found, she also wrote: 'It just feels like he hasn't called me in too long. I hear him singing. I hear him laugh.'
