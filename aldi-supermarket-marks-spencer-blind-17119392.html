Aldi and M&amp;S steaks put through blind taste test so which wins?

You don’t always have to spend a fortune to get hold of quality steak.

Aldi’s Aberdeen Angus Sirloin came out on top during a blind taste test.

The meaty offering retails at just £19.78 per kg, which is significantly cheaper to other alternatives on the market.

Channel 4’s Supershoppers recently revealed their verdict on Aldi’s premium steak.

The popular food product has been matured for 36 days, giving it a more intense flavour.

This process seems to have been worth the extra effort, as the sirloin cut scored highly in a taste test.

Reviewers believe the budget supermarket’s Aberdeen Angus Sirloin steaks are more delicious than M&S’.

As Marks and Spencer offering is 40% pricier, this is bound to be great news for bargain hunting Brits.

Taste testers were fans of the M&S sirloin, which they described as “nice and soft”.

While they reacted positively to the premium product, they were bigger fans of Aldi’s.

One reviewer gushed: “It’s much better – that tastes so much more than the other one.”

While the German supermarket beat Marks and Spencer on sirloin steaks, rib eye was a different story.

Supershoppers said M&S rib eye was much better on the palate.

A foodie said: “You can taste some aging on that.”

Another agreed that Marks and Spencer’s offering was better.

Of the Aldi steak, they commented: “It’s tasty but nowhere near as good as (M&S).”

Supershoppers can be viewed on demand on the All4 website.
