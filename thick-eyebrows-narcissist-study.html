Thick eyebrows linked to narcissism Study suggests that if you have busy brows, you are a narcis

Getty Images

Bad news if you have thick, groomed eyebrows because it could very well mean that you are a narcissist, at least that's what one study conducted by the University of Toronto has concluded.

On the outset, the study aimed to discover if one facial feature in particular hinted at a narcissistic personality. The researchers took head shots of participants and had them complete the Narcissistic Personality Inventory (which is a standardised test).

Then, a second group of participants judged the set of pictures to decide who was the most self-important based solely on appearance. Singled-out areas of the face were also shown to the second group to help the researchers determine which factors made people think that others were self important.

Getty Images

"We found that people could accurately judge others' narcissism from the upper half of the face — more specifically, the eyebrows alone," Miranda Giacomin, lead author of the study told Allure.

The researchers concluded that "eyebrow distinctiveness" was directly related to the individual's level of narcism. So the bolder, bushier and more prominent the brow, the more narcissistic you probably are.

"Thick brows are trendy right now," Giacomin said. "A person's preference for thick brows may be related to their desire to be attractive and popular."
