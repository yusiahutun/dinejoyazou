The story of Geri Halliwell's Union Jack dress

Getty

It has been 20 years since Geri Halliwell wore the now infamous Union Jack dress to perform at the Brit Awards. If that doesn't make you feel old, we're not sure what will.

The dress has gone down in history – along with Liz Hurley's safety-pin frock and Princess Diana's "revenge" gown – as one of the most memorable fashion moments of all time.

In celebration, here are five facts you may not have known about the dress.

1. It started out life as a black Gucci dress

Emma Poole, who managed the creative styling and marketing for the Spice Girls, explained that the band was working with quite a famous stylist at the time who had secured "amazing" dresses for the Brits. Halliwell, however, thought hers was too "boring".

"Two nights before, Geri said, 'You know what, Emma, I'm not really comfortable with the dress I'm wearing, I've got a much better idea," Poole later told The Telegraph. "I'm going around to my sister's, she's got these great Union Jack tea-towels, I'm going to make a dress.'"

She also made history.

2. It became the most expensive piece of pop-star clothing ever sold

Just one year after wearing the Union Jack dress, Halliwell sold it at a Sotheby's auction in London. It was bought by Peter Morton for £41,300 as a piece of pop memorabilia to be displayed at the Hard Rock Café in Las Vegas and Halliwell gave all the proceeds to a children's cancer charity.

Getty

3. In 2010, it helped the Spice Girls to win the 'Most Memorable Performance of 30 Years' at the Brit Awards

Which is not a bad coup for a dress made of tea towels. Fellow bandmate Mel B said the Union Jack moment was what made her realise just how good Halliwell was at dealing with the media.

"That was the first time I realised how brilliant she was," Brown said. "She had a real sense of what would catch on in the press. But she never really shared her cleverness with the rest of us."

Getty

4. She resurrected the look twice

For the Spice Girls reunion tour in 2007 and for the Olympics Closing Ceremony, Halliwell tapped into the famous creation.

For the tour, Roberto Cavalli recreated the look; this time it was a little longer and made out of rhinestones and Swarovski crystals. Halliwell said she liked the dress so much that she decided to keep it.

Getty

For the Olympics Closing Ceremony, Halliwell wore a short red dress with a full skirt and a Union Jack flag at the back.

Getty

5. In 2012, Halliwell designed a clothing range inspired by the dress

Fifteen years after it first made headlines, Halliwell teamed up with the high-street store Next to create a clothing line based on the dress. It featured dresses, bikinis and tops emblazoned with the flag.
