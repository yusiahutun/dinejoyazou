Almada Label is the new Quiet Luxury name you need to know

Each month, we look into an exciting and innovative label that is taking the fashion world by storm in our regular feature, #TheBrand. This time, we turn the spotlight on Almada Label, known best for its elevated knitwear.

Whether it’s Mob Wives or Grandpacore, there is a new style-related TikTok trend pretty much every week and frankly, we should probably not pay much attention to them. However, there is one Internet-derived fashion aesthetic that has actual staying power, and that is Quiet Luxury. The movement – which we wouldn’t actually consider a trend at all – is about dressing in elevated classics that you invest in for life. Of course, The Row and Loro Piana are always mentioned when this topic arises, but there are plenty of great brands known for these luxury basics that sit at a slightly more reasonable price point too. One of these is Almada Label, which hails from Finland and is all about high-quality minimalism.

Almada Label

Almada Label

“We established the brand in 2020 to address a longstanding quest in our fashion journey – finding timeless luxury knitwear that married quality craftsmanship with accessible pricing,” Alexa Dagmar – who set up the brand alongside Linda Juhola – tells me of the label. The collections are made up of soft cashmere cardigans and jumpers, knit tops and beautiful coats in a muted colour palette, with prices ranging from around £200 to £700. “Our aim is simple: to provide our customers with high-quality, luxury pieces at a reasonable price point. We keep our profit margins lower than the industry standard, ensuring that our customers can access exceptional, reasonably priced fashion. As a result, we've made the deliberate choice not to have sales or discounts, consistently maintaining our commitment to offering the best value.”

Almada Label

Dagmar and Juhola both had backgrounds in the digital marketing industry, working with various brands, as well as building up followings of their own as bloggers. Seeing a gap in the market for what they call “everyday luxury at a reasonable price”, they set up the business out of their homes during the pandemic, while both of them were pregnant. “The journey was far more challenging than we anticipated,” Juhola says, explaining that the complexities of production and the ever-evolving landscape of logistics and technology, was difficult to manage, but once everything was in place, they were able to focus on what they really believe in, which is high-quality investment pieces, and total comfort.

“Everyday luxury, to us as two working moms, is synonymous with the simple pleasure of a good night's sleep, which is often a rare treat in our busy lives,” Juhola says. “We also believe that wearing the softest clothes plays a pivotal role in our comfort and overall sense of wellbeing. Being comfortable really radiates an air of elegance.”

Comfort, ease and minimalism are all associated with Quiet Luxury of course, but are also indicative of Scandinavian style, which has been a huge influence on Almada’s designs.

Almada Label

“It definitely weaves its way into our pieces,” Dagmar says of Scandi style. “Our knitwear embraces clean lines, muted tones, and a commitment to quality craftsmanship, all while maintaining respect for the environment. These are all definitely a reflection of the serene and minimalist Scandinavian aesthetic.”

Almada Label

Almada Label

With plenty of exciting projects in the pipeline (including more size inclusivity, physical retail and new product lines), Dagmar and Juhola feel committed to their vision which, although it might be ‘on trend’ right now, has far more longevity in it than most TikTok moments.

“Quiet Luxury is so much more than just a trend; it possesses lasting appeal. If we examine style icons from previous decades, we can see that elements of Quiet Luxury have always been present, social media has merely given it a name. The minimalist designs and neutral colour palettes provide an elegant canvas for personal expression, making it accessible and adaptable.”
