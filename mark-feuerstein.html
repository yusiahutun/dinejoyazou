Mark Feuerstein is a popular American Actor, Writer, Producer, and Director. Mark Feuerstein is widely known for his character of Josh in the romantic comedy television series titled Sex and The City in 1999.

Early Life and Childhood

Similarly, he is American by nationality and Christian by religion. Moreover, he belongs to the Jewish ethnicity and his zodiac sign is Gemini.

He was born to a lawyer Harvey Feuerstein (father) and a school teacher Audrey Feuerstein (mother). Likewise, he has a brother named Eric Feuerstein.

Education

In terms of his educational background, he has done his schooling at Dalton High School in New York. Later, he graduated from Princeton University. Additionally, he attended acting classes at the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art and A’Ecole Philippe Gaulier, Paris.

Career and Professional Life

Moving on to his career, he made his television debut as a cop on the daytime soap opera titled Loving. When director Nancy Meyers was casting for What Women Want, her daughter recognized him from the 1998 film titled Practical Magic and insisted on his casting.

Similarly, he gained popularity after making a guest appearance in Season 2 of Sex and the City by playing an ophthalmologist named Josh. He also appeared as a lawyer in Season 3 of The West Wing and reprised the role in Season 6.

Furthermore, he reunited with Practical Magic co-star Sandra Bullock in the film titled Two Weeks’ Notice in 2002. In 2003, he was named one of the “50 Most Beautiful People” by People magazine. He debuted in the Crackle web series titled The Hustler in January 2009.

Likewise, from 2009 to 2016, he starred as the lead in the USA Network television series titled Royal Pains. He also starred as rogue CIA operative Jacob Ness in the Fox revival of the original hit series titled Prison Break in the spring of 2017.

Additionally, he co-created and stars in the CBS sitcom titled 9JKL which premiered in October 2017 with his wife, Dana Klein. It received negative reviews from critics upon its initial release, with a 13% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes. The series was canceled after one season on May 12, 2018.
