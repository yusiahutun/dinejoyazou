Soipan Tuya: Environment CS Sues Husband for Child Support, Demands KSh 425k Monthly Upkeep

  • Environment CS Soipan Tuya and Stephene Ole Kudate sired two children in the course of their union
  • Unknown hiccups would, however, dent their marriage, Tuya now moving to court to seek monthly child support from Kudate
  • She wants Kudate, a former majority leader in the Narok county assembly, to be compelled to send KSh 425,000 cumulative monthly for child support

Nairobi - Environment, Climate Change and Forestry CS Soipan Tuya is contemplating starting a legal suit against her estranged husband over child upkeep.

In court papers spotted by this publication, the CS wants Stephen Ole Kudate to be put under the obligation to remit to her KSh 425,000 monthly as support for their two children.

The monies would account for school fees at Rusinga Schools, which has been capped at KSh 450,000, Tuya is now asking for half of it from her former husband.

The remainder is KSh 100,000 for the children's entertainment needs and another KSh 100,000 for upkeep and maintenance.

Inside Soipan Tuya's affidavit

Besides the upkeep money, the CS wants the court to bar Kudate from abusing her and the children either physically, emotionally, verbally or psychologically, and also insulting and issuing threats of whatever nature against them.

Further, Tuya wanted Kudate to be barred from interfering with her place of work, going near her, and even making phone calls to her, as well as keeping tabs on her life.

"That the Honourable Court be pleased to issue a permanent injunction restraining the defendant by himself, servants, agents and representatives from accessing, entering or in another manner interfering with the plaintiff and her children's peaceful enjoyment of plaintiff's properties," read partly papers addressed to the court.

The affidavit was sworn on September 20, 2023.

Properties Tuya wants hubby barred from

The properties she wants the estranged husband to be blocked from include residential houses in Kileleshwa Estate, Karen Estate in Nairobi and Naroosoru in Narok South.

In the affidavit, Soipan has listed the properties solely under her name.

"That the Honourable Court be pleased to issue a permanent anti-molestation order/ protection order/ non-molestation order directed to and restraining the defendant either by himself, the servants or agents from accessing the plaintiff home, children's school or anywhere else except as shall be designated and supervised by this honourable court," read the papers further.

Who is Soipan Tuya?

Tuya made history as the first Masaai woman to be in Cabinet.

She is the daughter of former Narok South MP Samson Ole Tuya and served as Narok woman representative for two five-year terms until 2022.
