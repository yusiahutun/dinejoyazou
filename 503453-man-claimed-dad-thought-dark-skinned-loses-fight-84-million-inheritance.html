Man Loses Fight for 84m Inheritance Claims Dad Thought He Was: &quot;Too Dark-Skinned&quot;

  • Sunny Pyburn, a 38-year-old man, lost a legal battle to claim a share of his father's 84 million inheritance
  • Sunny claims that his father, Bill Pyburn, did not believe that he was his son because of his dark skin complexion
  • Sunny claimed his father subjected him to "horrific" physical and emotional abuse as a child, often calling him racial slurs

Sunny Pyburn, aged 38, has lost a court case for a share of his late father's KSh 84 million inheritance after alleging physical and emotional abuse as a child.

Sunny was born after Bill's affair with his psychologist, Gita Deb, who was Asian, and Bill suspected that Sunny was not his biological son due to his skin tone.

Sunny's father left everything to wife

Sunny claimed that his father subjected him to "horrific" physical and emotional abuse as a child, affecting him to date.

Bill entirely cut Sunny out of his will before he passed on in 2020, with his home going to his wife, Jackie Pyburn.

Bill and Jackie never divorced, and they had lived together in the house since 1969.

Sunny launched a "moral claim" against Jackie for a share of his dad's estate, citing the cruel abuse he suffered that has affected him today.

Judge refutes claim

Marie-Clare Bleasdale threw out his claim following a trial at Central London County Court after she found that Jackie's need was more significant than Sunny's.

She accepted that Sunny had been subjected to severe abuse but recognised that Jackie was a victim due to her husband's unfaithfulness.

She also agreed Sunny's current way of living was not ideal, but Jackie's need was greater than Sunny's, and he was old enough to stand on his own two feet.

Angry father

Bill began an affair with a psychologist in the early 1980s, ultimately leaving his wife to live with his lover.

Sunny didn't support his father, and in retaliation, Bill changed his will immediately, leaving his house and wealth to Jackie.
