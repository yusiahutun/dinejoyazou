Three Florida middle schoolers charged with plot to kill classmates 'based on Columbine and Sandy Ho

Two 13-year-old girls and a 12-year-old boy have been charged with plotting to kill classmates at their Florida school after researching the massacres at Sandy Hook and Columbine.

The trio planned to cause 'chaos' at Sleepy Hill Middle School, according to Lakeland Police, in a plot unearthed by a guidance counselor.

The friends had not purchased any materials but had allegedly mapped out their plan to set off multiple explosives across the school campus, according toFox News.

The murder-plot was allegedly discovered by the counselor as she looked through a notebook belong to one of the female teens.

Alleged plot: Three students have been charged with an alleged plot to set off explosives across Sleepy Hill Middle School, Lakeland, Florida

'I think we were very fortunate, because if they were able to acquire some of the materials, they could have harmed themselves just by trying to experiment on that, trying to assemble the device,' Captain Greg Policastro, with the Lakeland Police Department, told the broadcaster.

'There was quite a bit of corroborating material discovered,' he added.

One of the girls said to be the ringleader of the plot was institutionalized on her arrest on Thursday following the police arrest of the other girl involved last week.

She had allegedly researched other mass killings and on a search of her bedroom they discovered research on how to make a bomb.

Officers don't believe the students would have had the know-how to successfully build such a device but the alleged conspiracy has caused concern across the community.

Captain John Thomason said in a statement to the Orlando Sentinel: 'The Lakeland Police Department and Polk County Public Schools take it very serious when potential threats to the safety of schools, and the children that attend them, come to our attention.

Shooting: The alleged ringleader - a 13-year-old girl - studied the Columbine High School shooting, according to police

Inspiration: The trio are accused of plotting to kill many across the school campus and one was said to be researching the Sandy Hook massacre

'The partnership we have with school officials will ensure that we do everything possible to keep our children and community safe from harm.'

'Because of the action of school personnel and the Lakeland Police Department, a potential crisis at this school may have been interrupted.'

There is no evidence that other students were involved in the plot and the trio's parents had no knowledge of what the children were considering.

The students were charged with three felonies - Conspiracy to Commit First Degree Murder, Conspiracy to Commit Aggravated Battery and Conspiracy to Construct Destructive Devices.

It is unclear whether they will be charged as adults of juveniles. 

A neighbor told ABC News one of the girls was bullied at school and frustrated that no one would help her. 

'Nobody would help her, from what I was told. She went to counseling, went to the teachers, everybody ignored her. She made that idle threat, but it was the wrong threat to make in this day and age.'
