24 Best Low-Maintenance Flowers and Plants Outdoors

Also known as swamp magnolia, this ornamental tree is a fuss-free choice for part shade and naturally moist, rich acidic soil.

Zones 5–10; native to the U.S.; deer resistant

Low-Maintenance Landscaping Tip: “Planting the right plant for the right environment will result in the most effortless maintenance for your plants,” says landscape designer Daniel McCurry of Father Nature Landscapes in Birmingham, Alabama, "For example, if you have an area with partial shade and wet soil, you choose sweetbay magnolia, inkberry, sweetspire, and roof iris." This plant list, given that it loves this environment, he explains, will thrive and require less maintenance than, say, an azalea, which likes a dry root system after establishment and will require lots of chemicals, cleaning, and care.
