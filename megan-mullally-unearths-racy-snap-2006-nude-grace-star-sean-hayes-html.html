Megan Mullally unearths RACY snap from 2006 with her NUDE Will &amp; Grace co-star Sean Hayes

NBC's Will & Grace returns from its holiday hiatus with the mid-season premiere on Thursday night, with series star Megan Mullally celebrating in a unique way.

The 60-year-old actress shared a throwback photo from 2006 on Instagram, which shows her fully-dressed in a black long-sleeved top and blue jeans, being caressed by her fully-nude co-star Sean Hayes.

As it turns out, this photo was actually lost several years ago, only to be found again and posted by Mullally to commemorate the midseason premiere.

And the risque snap mirrors the iconic photograph of the Beatles frontman John Lennon and his wife Yoko Ono by Annie Leibovitz from 1980.

'#WillAndGrace had just ended- or so we thought- and i had the idea to do this photo with sean whom i had loved so much and worked so closely with for those eight years, to commemorate our very symbiotic partnership,' she continued. 

'I had a photo shoot scheduled for something else with the photographer @jackguyphotography, and we tacked it onto the end of that shoot,' she said, before revealing the big twist.

'What better way to get y’all’s attention because WILL & GRACE COMES BACK TONIGHT AT 9:30/8:30c,' she said, plugging the hit sitcom's return.

'Thanks to my friend and partner in crime @seanhayes for doing this photo with me and for being so unbelievably funny, talented, hard-working, and beyond creative. xxo,' Mullally concluded.

Will & Grace made its triumphant return with its 10th Season in the fall of 2017, and it was a huge hit right away.

The sitcom marked NBC's highest-rated sitcom debut in six years, and was already picked up for a second season before the first debuted.

NBC renewed the sitcom again for a third season back in March for 18 episodes, while extending the second season from 13 episodes to 18.

During its original run, Will & Grace ran for eight seasons between 1998 and 2006, starring Debra Messing as Grace, Eric McCormack as Will along with Mullally and Hayes.

Rumors of a new series sparked in 2016 when Messing, McCormack, Mullally and Hayes reunited for a new politically-charged short.
