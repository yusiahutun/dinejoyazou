Young girl is orphaned after lawyer dad and executive mom both die in PLANE CRASH: Dad recently rece

A father heroically tried to drag his wife from the burning wreck of his plane after they crashed, orphaning their daughter.

Barrett Ellis only earned his pilot license weeks before his Beechcraft Sierra crashed near Wiley Post Airport in Oklahoma City, killing him and his wife Megan.

The financial lawyer managed to escape the plane as it caught fire and desperately tried to free his wife, but suffered horrific burns and died in hospital.

'He had some burn injuries... we quickly began to cut some of his clothing off to try to cool the burns the best that we could. Just an unfortunate scene,' Oklahoma City Fire Captain Scott Douglas said.

Mr Ellis' plane took off from the airport at 10.18am (CST) on Sunday and only spent seconds in the air before it went down.

The couple's friends will hold a vigil on Wednesday to support their 10-year-old daughter, who was not on board the plane.

Ms Ellis' parents Rick and Annette Brown live in nearby McCloud, OK, and frequently spend time with their granddaughter.

The family proudly posed inside the 1968 red single-engine plane when Mr Ellis earned his license at Route 66 Flight School on October 7.

'This was not an easy thing to do: hours in the cockpit, hours in the books, two examinations, and 13 months of making life work around trips out to the airport. I'm incredibly grateful to my excellent instructor and my supportive wife,' he wrote.

'And wait, there's more: We have an airplane. The pretty red girl you see below is our family's new ticket to adventure. 

'If you thought you were safe from the Ellis family, you better think again. We could show up at your doorstep at any time.'

Flight logs show Mr Ellis completed a 25-minute flight from Wiley Post Airport on Saturday afternoon.

Husband and wife both studied at Oklahoma University and Mr Ellis became in-house counsel for the university's foundation in 2020 after a decade in financial law.

Devastated friends of the couple shared memories and tributes online as they learned of their deaths.

Ms Ellis' high school friend Carrie Winford recalled how Ms Ellis clogged at the school talent show 

'Some people were judgy and critical, but by the end of her performance they were amazed at her energy and talent,' she wrote.

'We were merely acquaintances, but there was something authentic about her that made you feel like it was okay to be yourself. She was brave, vibrant and creative, and every time I saw her, it looked like she was having fun.

'I wish I had told her that I admired her many years ago, as I watched her fluff her bangs in the 90s at Falls Creek camp.'

'While so many memories have been running through my mind as I've begun to grieve, the moment that I keep coming back to is the pure joy and excitement we felt as we held hands, jumping on the bleachers screaming as the Sooners made a last second touchdown,' she wrote.

'While I miss her so much already, I will forever be grateful for her mentorship and friendship.'

Local Methodist pastor Adam Ricks was a frequent travel companion of Mr Ellis and explained his adventurous streak

'He was always up to do the next fun thing, road trip to Mexico for a triathlon, hiking through the mountains of Peru, just mountain biking around Lake Arcadia, or having a way to thoughtful theological conversation,' he wrote.

'He and Megan were there for me in good times and bad. They packed more into their short lives than most people do in long ones.'
