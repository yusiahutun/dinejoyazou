How to clean gold jewellery

Caring for gold jewellery can be confusing. With different karat weights, and depending on the gems set within it, there isn’t a one-size-fits-all approach when it comes to cleaning. So, we have rounded up the top tips from a number of jewellery experts on the best way to clean your gold jewellery.

The most important thing? Make sure you’re aware of the metal and gems in each piece, and you can proceed safely from there.

Don’t worry about karat weight

The ‘karat weight’ refers to the proportion of pure gold vs. alloy in the metal, but there’s no need to be concerned by this when it comes to cleaning. “The higher the karat, the softer the metal,” explains Cece Fein Hughes, founder of Cece Jewellery, who works in 18-karat gold and enamel. “This difference, however, is only really felt by the goldsmiths bending, cutting, and shaping it into jewellery. As a wearer, there isn’t much difference on how it should be cleaned, but like anything valuable you own, you need to look after it! If cared for properly, your jewellery will last generations.”

Polish 9-karat gold regularly

You might, however, notice a difference in how regularly different karat weights need to be cleaned. “The lower the grade of gold the more it will tarnish, so if you like wearing 9-karat gold then it's handy to keep a gold cleaning cloth to hand so that when it starts to fade a little bit, you can buff it back to a lovely bright hue,” says Lucy Crowther, founder of London-based brand Minka, who sets gleaming gold with a rich array of coloured gems.

Plain gold is easy to clean

“To keep gold looking clean and sparkling, give it a regular clean in some warm soapy water; I like to let it soak for a few mins to help lift off any dirt,” suggests Dima Nawbar, co-founder of Beirut-based brand L’Atelier Nawbar. “If you have a little ultrasonic cleaner at home, this gives wonderful results as it lifts the dirt from all the hard-to-get to bits. After a vacation when you have been wearing sunscreen and swimming in the sea or a pool, it's best to give it a quick clean to get any chemicals or salt off,” she advises.

Take extra care with gem-set pieces

Cleaning becomes a little trickier when gold jewels feature precious gemstones, especially soft and porous materials such as pearls and emeralds. “Steer clear of pearls, opals, and emeralds when it comes to ultrasonic cleaners,” says Crowther. “The best way to clean your precious pieces is to use warm to hot (not boiling) water, a little bit of fairy liquid or something similar and a soft bristled toothbrush. If you are in any doubt, take it to your trusted jeweller and they can clean it for you.”

Clean enamel cautiously

The same rule applies for enamelled gold jewellery. “Enamel is essentially glass, so you must not put your enamel jewellery in an ultrasonic cleaner in case the glass cracks,” explains Fein Hughes. “We have had incidents when clients have boiled their pieces and burnt the enamel straight off, and harsh soap can cause stains or discolouration,” concurs Nawbar. Don’t, however, let anxiety put you off from wearing your collection. “If you love it and care for it, it will last,” says Nawbar. “Jewellery is made to be worn, so most importantly, just enjoy it.”
