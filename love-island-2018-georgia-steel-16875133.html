Love Island's Georgia Steel has VERY famous dad

The Love Island 2018 bombshell has come clean about her family ties to fame.

Georgia's biological father is professional jockey Rodney Lappin.

Rodney was famous on the racing scene and Love Island Georgia remembers being approached by people who knew him.

Sadly, Rodney was absent from a lot of her childhood, after leaving the family home when Georgia was just three.

Love Island's new gal Georgia Steel View gallery

She told The Sun: "People would be like: 'Oh my god, you're Rod's daughter.'

"That's hard. That's a hard thing to go through.

"All I wanted to believe in my head was that Andy was my biological dad. And when someone would come up to me and say something like that, it would really hurt me."

Rodney only started trying to contact his daughter in her teens but when he was approached for comment, he said he wished his estranged daughter "every success and happiness".

Georgia is adamant she doesn't want a relationship with her father and praised her mother and step-father Andrew.

The drama student even admitted that her famous "I'm loyal, babe" catchphrase was in part because of them.

She added: "I had to be strong for my mum, even at the age of four, I was my mum’s rock, I gave my mum meaning. It has made me stronger, it really has.

"If I'm honest with you, the whole loyalty thing, that is something my mum has brought me up with. To always be loyal to whoever you're with."

Georgia has been slowly assimilating into civilian life since leaving Love Island with her boyfriend Sam Bird, 25.

The couple have moved in together and have been doing various club appearances.

She continued: "It made me who I am today. Without having those experiences as a child, that rocky upbringing, it wouldn't have made me as strong.

"I just want to focus on my relationship with Sam and my career now."
