Rivea London review, Bulgari Hotel

The buzz

Since the Bulgari Hotel opened in 2014, it's become known as one of the grandest, most luxurious places to stay in London. Needless to say, it's a requisite that its restaurant has to be of the same calibre, so Alain Ducasse was enlisted in 2014 to head up Rivea London – the hotel's basement dining option – serving guests and discerning locals a combination of Italian and French cuisine. New to the restaurant is its chestnut menu, which tastes as autumnal as falling leaves look.

Ricotta ravioli and delicate chestnut velouté

The lowdown

In terms of interiors, Rivea is traditional luxe – wooden panelling and plush chairs – infused with modernity, cue the sweeping staircase and beaded curtain. Surfaces and mirrors gleam, but it is still relatively relaxed for a formal restaurant. The new menu is a reference to the French Riviera's chestnut harvest, which is celebrated at festivals with much drinking, eating and merry-making. Rivea's autumn menu is an homage to the area's much-loved seasonal produce.

Slow-cooked boar shoulder

Menu highlights

Unless you have a nut allergy, the menu is unarguably good. Four courses of chestnuts might sound overbearing, but the ingredient is added with such subtlety that you're left feeling impressed by its versatility rather than bored. The ricotta ravioli and delicate chestnut velouté starter was wonderfully warming, and the perfect size for a pasta-based appetiser. The roasted sea scallops with pumpkin and chestnut was beautifully fresh, but our heart belongs to the slow-cooked boar shoulder, which was as tender and guiltily rich as imaginable. Save space for dessert – pear and chestnut vacherin, a fiendishly good mix of cream and fruit.

Perfect for

A decadent autumnal evening when sustenance, quality and warmth are priorities.

Book it

Bvlgari Hotel London, Bulgari Hotel, 171 Knightsbridge, London SW7, Rivealondon.com
