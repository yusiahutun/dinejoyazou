Many historical theories explain the origin of surnames. It is believed that surnames originated in England. English barons documented some surnames in the 11th century. English surnames began as a way of identifying an individual but were not necessarily inherited. They were associated with the person's trade/profession, father's name, place of birth, or physical features.

How did last names start?

Some historical theories claim family names may have developed among the nobles in China and spread to commoners. Clan names indicating matrilineal heritage emerged in China around 4000–3000 BC.

When did last names start?

People began using family names in the later Middle Ages (around the beginning of the 11th century). The process was completed by the end of the 16th century.

Why do Filipinos have Spanish last names?

Spanish last names are common in the Philippines because of the Spanish conquest and the implementation of a Spanish naming system in the Philippine Islands. After the Spanish conquest of the Philippine islands, many early Christianized Filipinos assumed religious-instrument or saint names.
