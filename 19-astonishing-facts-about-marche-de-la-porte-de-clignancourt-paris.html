FAQs

Q: What is the Marché de la Porte de Clignancourt?

A: The Marché de la Porte de Clignancourt is a famous flea market located in the north of Paris, France. It is one of the largest and oldest flea markets in the world, known for its vast array of vintage items, antiques, clothing, and collectibles.

Q: When is the market open?

A: The market is open on Saturdays, Sundays, and Mondays. It typically starts early in the morning and runs until mid-afternoon. However, it’s a good idea to check the specific opening hours as they may vary depending on the season and weather conditions.

Q: What can I find at the Marché de la Porte de Clignancourt?

A: The market offers a wide range of goods, including antique furniture, vintage clothing, accessories, artwork, books, records, and much more. You can also find unique and quirky items that are not easily found elsewhere.

Q: Are the prices negotiable?

A: Yes, bargaining is a common practice at the Marché de la Porte de Clignancourt. Vendors often set their prices higher initially, expecting customers to negotiate for a lower price. It is recommended to brush up on your bargaining skills and be prepared for some friendly haggling.

Q: Are there any food options at the market?

A: Yes, there are several food stalls and cafes scattered throughout the market where you can grab a quick bite or enjoy a leisurely meal. You can find a variety of cuisines ranging from local French delicacies to international fare.

Q: Is it necessary to bring cash or do vendors accept card payments?

A: While some vendors may accept card payments, it is advisable to bring cash as not all vendors have card payment facilities. Additionally, having cash on hand allows for easier negotiation and flexibility during transactions.
