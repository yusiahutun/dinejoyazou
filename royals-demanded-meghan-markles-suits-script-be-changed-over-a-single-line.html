Royals 'demanded' Meghan Markle's Suits script be changed over a single line

Meghan Markle’s Suits will be always iconic but the director of the show has revealed some surprising details that viewers were not aware of all this time!

The show’s creator, Aaron Korsh has recently claimed that the Royal family apparently requested some minor changes in the script when Markle started dating Prince Harry.

Royals ‘demanded’ Meghan Markle’s Suits script be changed over a single line

In a new interview, TV Suits maker Aaron Korsh made claims saying the royal family expressed opinions on some stuff about Meghan Markle‘s lines on the show.

He said: “I will say, and I think Harry put this in the book, because I heard people talking about it — [the royal family] weighed in on some stuff. Not many things, by the way, but a few things that we wanted to do and couldn’t do, and it was a little irritating.”

He explained: “I remember one was a particular line of dialogue and, look, I’ll just say what the line was. My wife’s family, when they have a topic to discuss that might be sensitive, they use the word, ‘poppycock.”

“The Royal Family did not want her saying the word”

Korsh explained that the particular line of Meghan (Rachel) in the show was part of a scene where Rachel is talking about her family.

He added: “So, in the episode, Mike and Rachel were going to have a thing, and as a nod to my in-laws, we were going to have her say, ‘My family would say poppycock.’ And the royal family did not want her saying the word. They didn’t want to put the word ‘poppycock’ in her mouth.”

Ultimately, Rachel’s dialogue was changed, and she ended up saying ‘bull***t’ rather than ‘poppycock.’

Meghan’s dating life and the timeline of Suits

The Duchess of Sussex starred in Suits from 2011 to 2018 and she started dating Prince Harry sometime in 2016. While Korsh is not sure about how the royal family got access to the scripts of Meghan’s show, he claimed: “I was aware that they were reading them because I got the feedback, but I don’t remember the process by which they got them.”

Korsh is clearly not happy about the situation as he added: “Whoever it was, they didn’t like having to tell me any more than I liked having to hear it.”
