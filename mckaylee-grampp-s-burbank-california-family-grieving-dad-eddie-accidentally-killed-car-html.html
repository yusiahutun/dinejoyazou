McKaylee Grampp's Burbank family grieving after dad Eddie accidentally killed her

A California family who have already experienced their share of hardships are mourning the loss of three-year-old McKaylee 'Boo' Grampp after she has accidentally killed by her father on August 12.

Eddie Grampp, who was diagnosed with an inoperable brain tumor a few months after McKaylee's birth and has been battling the aggressive cancer for almost three years, struck the girl with his car.

After she was accidentally hit in the parking garage of their multifamily home in Burbank, McKaylee was treated for her injuries at the scene and taken to the hospital, where she eventually died.

Eddie and his wife Mary, both 32, have two young sons, aged five and six, and they are expecting a baby girl

Eddie and his wife Mary, both 32, have two young sons, aged five and six, and they are expecting a baby girl who is due in November, according to the Burbank Leader. 

The Grampps have turned to their Mormon faith

Before their first son was born, Mary had three ectopic pregnancies and she almost died because of one of them, the Deseret News reported.

The Grampps, who are members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, are attempting to use their faith and the help of friends to help deal with the massive 'hole in their hearts'.

Mary said: 'She is still close.

'We just keep reminding ourselves of what we know to be true, that we will see her again and she's in a good place with Heavenly Father.

'She has been a blessing in so many lives.'

Eddie, who still carries a plastic egg McKaylee used to 'cook' him breakfast on the day she died, credits his now-deceased daughter with giving him the will to battle his cancer for so long. 

He said: ''She gave me a goal to fight for.

'She gave me love and encouraged me all along the way.'

The Nickelodeon computer animator's new goal is to see the birth of his second baby girl. 

Mary said of her daughter: 'She is still close. She has been a blessing in so many lives'

Eddie still carries a plastic egg that little McKaylee used to 'cook' him breakfast on the day she died

Friends set up an online fundraiser for the family that has already raised more than $50,000. 

The creator of the fundraiser wrote: 'This is a family that even during their financial and emotional struggles have done everything in their power to be of service in their community. 

'They are kind and generous people to a fault, and we feel that in light of this most recent travesty they are desperately in need of that kindness and generosity being returned to them. 

'We wish to lend support to these sweet people who have been given more to bear than any human should ever have to bear.'
