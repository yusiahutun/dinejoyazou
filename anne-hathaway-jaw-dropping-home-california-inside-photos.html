WeCrashed star Anne Hathaway's jaw-dropping California home is totally unexpected - see inside

Anne Hathaway lives in complete luxury in Calfornia with her husband Adam Shulman and their sons Jonathan and Jack.

MORE: Anne Hathaway wows in stunning dress with very revealing detail

The WeCrashed actress is very private about her family abode and prefers to keep it off social media, but late last year she did give fans a peek inside the Swiss chalet-style property in Architectural Digest – and the images are utterly stunning. The charming home features wooden décor throughout and outside there's a living space that has the most breathtaking mountain views.

WATCH: Anne Hathaway gives a rare look inside her cosy home

The spacious residence was designed in conjunction with interior designer Pamela Shamshiri, with the couple having some surprising inspiration for their décor and color palette.

The peach and burgundy tones in the entrance and music room were inspired by a favorite Gucci shirt, while Rihanna’s famous imperial-yellow cape from the 2015 Met Gala helped to influence the golden fabrics and carpet in the music room and den.

"She brought a sense of sophistication, magic, and fun to the whole process," Anne told Architectural Digest of Pamela's involvement.

We love the look of Anne's picture-perfect home in the Californian hills

It was love at first sight for Anne and Adam. Speaking to Architectural Digest, Adam revealed: "The minute we came up the driveway and saw this incredible panoramic view unfold in front of us, we were hooked."

The music room is one of Anne and Jonathan’s favorite spots in the house and has been kitted out with an early 20th-century disco ball from a Turkish spa, a piano, a sparkly Yves Klein Monogold table, and comfortable seating for the couple’s guests.

Anne explained: "This room has been a long-held fantasy of ours – a place where the people we love can gather and our musician friends can play. It’s the heart of the house."

The property's exterior is just as spectacular 

The outside of the property is just as striking as the interiors as the home has stone and wooden exteriors, giving it a very country-cottage feel.

 A large tree shades the property, providing the stars with privacy while their mountainside position means they are not overlooked by anyone.
