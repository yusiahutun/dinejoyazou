Bakery behind Tom Cruise's Christmas cake says he 'kept us in business'

One of the hottest lists to be on in Hollywood is Tom Cruise's gift list: Every Christmas, the Scientologist and Mission Impossible star gifts friends, other stars, and executives a coveted cake from a California bakery.

Affectionately referred to as the 'Tom Cruise cake' by many of the stars who've snagged one, the White Chocolate Coconut Bundt Cake from Doan's Bakery, in Woodland Hills, California, has been described as 'amazing' and 'unbelievable.'

And in the midst of the pandemic, Doan's Bakery's owners credit Cruise's huge repeat order for being one of the big reasons they've stayed in business.

Tasty: It's made with 'a ring of moist, luxurious coconut bundt cake,' 'chunks of sweet white chocolate,' a layer of cream cheese frosting, and toasted coconut flakes on top

The cake, which is available on Goldbelly for $99 and at the bakery's Woodland Hills location for $50, was created in 1984 by bakery founder Karen Doan, 78, who runs the shop with her son Eric. 

It's made with 'a ring of moist, luxurious coconut bundt cake,' 'chunks of sweet white chocolate,' a layer of cream cheese frosting, and toasted coconut flakes on top.  

It's one of several sweets the bakery sells — but it's the one that Cruise and his Odin Productions company send out every year.

In the past, Cruise's gift recipients have included Kirsten Dunst, Henry Cavill, Rosie O'Donnell, Barbara Walters, Cobie Smulders, Jimmy Fallon, James Corden, and Angela Bassett.

And in a year when food industry business have been suffering, the Doans are grateful to Cruise for being so loyal. 

'This year has been a really rough ride,' Eric Doan told Yahoo. 'In March and April, we wondered if we were even going to be able to stay open.

Thrilled: This year, Rosie O'Donnell showed off her gift box from Cruise on Instagram

Pleased: Cruise's Mission: Impossible - Fallout costar Henry Cavill called it 'the most decadent, the most amazing, the most surprising cake, to receive from someone like Tom Cruise'

'Now, I’m spun like a top,' he said of the holiday season, adding that Cruise's order is 'honestly is one of the things that kept us alive' during the pandemic.

'There were years they kept us in business,' he added. 

It's unknown how many cakes Cruise sends out each year, but several stars have spoken out about getting one.

Most recently, Rosie O'Donnell shared the gift she received, a brown cardboard box with a beautiful gold bow, a reindeer ornament, and a gift tag: 'Warmest wishes to you this Holiday Season.'

'Christmas is here when Tommy’s gift shows up,' Rosie captioned the image on Instagram.

Kirsten Dunst, who starred with Cruise in 1994's Interview with a Vampire, told Graham Norton that the actor still 'gives me a cake every Christmas — we call it the Cruise Cake at my house.'

Lucky! Not all of Cruise's recipients are famous; he also sends cakes to producers, journalists, and executives

Special: A few recipients have shown off the cake on Instagram. Cruise's production company raps them with ribbon and adds a reindeer ornament

Fan: Cobie Smulders, who starred with Cruise in Jack Reacher: Never Go Back, discussed the 'Tom Cruise deliciousness,' calling the time it comes 'the most glorious time of the year.'

'It's the best coconut cake I've ever had in my life,' she said. 'It's really good and we get it every Christmas. All our family is like, "Cruise Cake's here!" It's like a major thing, we all devour it that night.'

Meanwhile, Henry Cavill — who acted with Cruise in 2018's Mission: Impossible - Fallout — gushed about it to Access Hollywood.

'I received this cake at Christmas time saying, "Dear Henry, Happy Christmas, from Tom Cruise." Which is an insane little thing to read,' he said.

'So I get the cake and I think it's a sort of super healthy coconut — the kind of thing that doesn't taste amazing. Nothing treat-like, and so I left it. I just left it in the fride,' he went on.

But when he finally tried it, it was 'the most decadent, the most amazing, the most surprising cake, to receive from someone like Tom Cruise. I loved it. Smashed it.'

And Cobie Smulders, who starred with Cruise in Jack Reacher: Never Go Back, discussed the 'Tom Cruise deliciousness' with Jimmy Fallon, calling the time it comes 'the most glorious time of the year.'

Voila! In 2013, Barbara Walters showed off her cake from Cruise on The View with Jenny McCarthy, Whoopi Goldberg, and Sherri Shepherd 

'This is from Tom Cruise,' she said. 'And everyone here got one, not just me. Isn't that nice?' 

'If you're lucky enough to be on this list,' Cobie said. 'It's so good, I leave it in my freezer and it lasts until, like March. I just slowly chip away at this thing. It's so good.' 

'You get anything from Tom Cruise, you eat, you put in the freezer, you save for a year,' Jimmy replied.

In 2013, Barbara Walters showed off her cake from Cruise on The View with Jenny McCarthy, Whoopi Goldberg, and Sherri Shepherd.

'This is from Tom Cruise,' she said. 'And everyone here got one, not just me. Isn't that nice?'   

Other people to get the cake include Sofia Vergara, Renée Zellweger, and Graham Norton.

'It's a nice cake,' Zellweger told Norton — whose crew ate up the cake before he could get any.

According to Yahoo, Cruise previously said that he sends cakes 'to everyone.' 

'And I wait for the calls,' he added, from people who gush about the cake. 'I’m like: Tell me about it.'

Hollywood fave: Sofia Vergara has also shared photos of herself with the cake, though it's unclear whether she got it from Cruise or purchased it herself

Eric Doan is aware that that the cake has taken on a life of its own.

'Over the years of Odin Productions gifting it, it’s taken on that moniker of the "Tom Cruise cake," if you will,' he said — but added that Diane Keaton loved it first. 

According to Yahoo, Keaton introduced the cake Katie Holmes when she was still married to Cruise while they were filming the movie Mad Money, which came out in 2008.

Cruise tasted it and clearly fell in love.

And though he has been buying the cake for years, Doan says they have never actually met the star.
