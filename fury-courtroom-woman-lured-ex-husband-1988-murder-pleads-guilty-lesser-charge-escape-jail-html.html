A woman accused in the murder of her 24-year-old ex-husband in 1988 has entered a guilty plea to a lesser charge, 24 years after his death.

Barbara Britton, 46, pleaded guilty to accessory-after-the-fact to first degree murder in a Broward, Florida courtroom Tuesday following the shooting death of David Jackson, 24.

Britton pled guilty to luring Jackson to a Dania Beach motel room where he was shot by her then-husband Michael Wolfe.

Plea deal: Barbara Britton, 46, pleaded guilty to accessory-after-the-fact to first degree murder of her then-24-year-old ex husband in 1988 to escape charges of first-degree murder

Wolfe was convicted of the crime and sentenced to life in prison in 2007, implicating Britton in the crime soon after when telling investigators Britton was a participant, reports the Sun Sentinel. 

In court Tuesday, Britton admitted to telling Jackson that her father wanted to meet up with him to discuss plans for their son when he was shot.

She has long maintained her innocence in his death arguing being in Arizona at the time of Jackson's death but pleading guilty to the lesser charge this week to escape a life sentence, if found guilty, of first-degree murder.

Victim: David Jackson, 24, (pictured) was shot in a motel room by Britton's then-husband but went missing for 15 years before his body was identified

Lured: Britton (Left), pleaded guilty to luring Jackson to the room he was shot in by telling him her father wanted to speak to him about the future of their son

'I was not present when David was killed,' Britton said before Broward Circuit Judge Raag Singhal according to the Sun Sentinel. 'I told David that my father wanted to meet with him to discuss his plans for our son… I never knew how or why David disappeared.'

Jackson's body was missing for 15 years before remains found a year after his death were identified through DNA as his own.

Britton's father, Harry, died before that identification in 1998 at the age of 64.

Arrests: Michael Wolfe was found guilty of shooting Jackson in 2007 and sentenced to life in prison where he told investigators Britton participated, prompting her arrest

With her plea Tuesday she was sentenced to two years of community control following eight years of probation in accordance to a deal made with prosecutors.

Jackson's mother, enraged by her former-daughter-in-law, spoke out in court Tuesday.

'You are a sociopath,' Judy Carlson addressed Britton in court according to the Sentinel. 'You did participate in that murder,' she asserted.

Family: Jackson's mother, pictured with him a year before his death, called Britton a sociopath in court, disbelieving her claims of having no participation in his death

Following Wolfe's conviction and claims against Britton, she was arrested as well and held for three years before granted $50,000 bail.

That allowance was provided after an inmate claimed that Wolfe had admitted to framing Britton for 'running her mouth,' according to the Sentinel.

Mrs Carlson still holds onto Wolfe's earlier claims against Britton, telling her in court: 'Michael [Wolfe] is where he should be in person ... your father is where he should be, and you will join him one day in Hell.'
