John Roberts: net worth, family, conservative, adopted children

If you like to keep up with the current news, you probably already know a bit about John Roberts. The news anchor has enjoyed a long and prosperous career in the news industry, especially on TV.

While John Roberts is a public figure, many people don't know about his personal life. Below is a detailed look at his life - from childhood, career beginnings and growth, and his relationship and children.

John Roberts profile summary

  • Name: John David Roberts
  • Date of birth: November 15, 1956 (64 years)
  • Place of birth: Toronto, Ontario in Canada
  • Education: Erindale College (University of Toronto)
  • Profession: Journalist
  • Partner: Kyra Philips (engaged 2010 - present)

Early life

John David Roberts was born on November 15, 1956, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, making him Canadian by birth. However, he later moved to the USA and became a citizen in 2001, thus making him Canadian-American.

Not much is known about Roberts' childhood. He grew up in Mississauga, Ontario, alongside his siblings – a brother and sister. At the young age of five, he experienced a tragedy when the family lost their father.

Roberts attended Erindale Secondary School before transferring to Lorne Park Secondary School, both in Mississauga. After completing his high school education, he enrolled at Erindale College – a satellite campus of the University of Toronto.

Career

John Roberts career in the news industry started at a young age. As a teenager growing up in Mississauga, he had a job selling subscriptions to Mississauga News through phone calls. He had been passionate about news and had applied to the paper, hoping to become a reporter. Unfortunately, he did not get it but was hired to work in subscription sales.

Career in radio

After enrolling in college, Roberts got a chance to fulfill his dreams of being a journalist. He worked in the campus radio station, CFRE-FM. After leaving school, he landed a job as a news anchor – his first professional job – with CFOS, a radio station based in Owen Sound, Ontario.

Roberts held several jobs at various radio stations in Ontario – CHYM, CJBK, and CHUM. He was primarily a news anchor, but he also worked as a DJ.

Entry into the TV world

After working for four years in the radio industry, Roberts changed sectors and went to TV. His first job was at CITY-TV, as a co-host of the show NewMusic with another Canadian TV. personality – Jeanne Beker.

For the next several years, he worked in the entertainment field, hosting several other shows such as Toronto Rocks and MuchMusic. However, he also sometimes dubbed as an entertainment reporter for the TV station. In 1987, Roberts changed career direction once again, leaving the entertainment field for news work.

He joined the CITY-TV news programme CityPulse as a news anchor for the 10 PM program. In 1989, Roberts left the station, moving to Miami, Florida in the US. He got a jog as a news anchor, and reporter for WCIX channel (now WFOR-TV), a CBS-owned TV station.

He held the position until 1990, when he returned home to Canada, this time as a co-host for the Canada AM show at CTV. In 1992, he returned to the US as an anchor for CBS News in New York. Initially, he worked as an anchor and newsreader for CBS Morning News, The Early Show, and CBS This Morning shows.

Roberts worked at the TV station for the next 13 years, holding various positions and responsibilities. He served as the anchor for the CBS Sunday Evening News for more than a decade. He also did journalism work, with one of the roles being the station's Chief White House Correspondent.

Other career highlights of Roberts include embedding with the US Marines during the Iraq invasion. He was also part of the coverage team for the supreme court nomination of John Roberts (now the Chief Justice).

Joining CNN

In 2006, Roberts joined the CNN network, where he continued his exemplary journalistic work. One of his first assignments was reporting on the Israeli/Hezbollah war. To this end, he embedded with an Israeli military unit operating in Lebanon.

He also took part in the coverage of the Iraqi war, anchoring the show This Week at War. At the time, he was a Senior CNN national correspondent based in Washington DC. He also worked as an anchor at the station and is famous for working in top shows such as American Morning and Anderson Cooper 3600.

Leaving CNN for Fox News

In 2011, Roberts left CNN for rival station Fox News as a national correspondent working out of Atlanta. Since joining the new channel, he has covered several events such as the Ebola Outbreak, Hurricane Irene, the Moore Tornado, and the murder trial of Robert Durst.

Roberts was also part of the Fox News political coverage team and held the position of Chief White House Correspondent from 2017 to 2021. Currently, he is an anchor and has been hosting the show American Reports.

Family

Unlike his professional life, not much is known about John Roberts family. His father died at a young age, with his mother also reported to have passed away around 2010. He also has two siblings – a brother and a sister, whom not much is known about.

Roberts is currently engaged to Kyra Philips, an ABC News Investigative Correspondent. Like her partner, she has had an exemplary career in journalism. She has covered wars, investigated sexual predators, covered Navy Seals training, and more.

Kyra and Roberts met when they were both working at CNN. They started a relationship and became engaged after Roberts divorced from his first wife, Michelle Roberts, in 2008.
