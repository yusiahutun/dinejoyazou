Lafayette Stone arrested by police tells officers he has hidden drugs in his bottom

Lafayette Stone (pictured) was arrested by officers amid reports of a carjacking

A man accused of an attempted car jacking told police 'I am trying to get the drugs out of my butt' after he was arrested, court documents reveal.

Lafayette Stone was detained by officers amid claims he had tried to take a car in the parking lot of a Walgreens pharmacy in Albuquerque, New Mexico.

After being arrested, he initially denied he had drugs on him before police found him reaching down the back of his pants, authorities say.

Court documents show that when officers asked him what he was doing, he said: 'I'm getting the drugs out of my butt.' Police say they recovered a 'white crystal substance' before asking whether he had any more.

He is then reported to have replied: 'I might have some up my anus.' A bag of black tar heroin was then recovered.

Police say the 37-year-old had earlier dragged one of two women he was carjacking 25 ft alongside the car as she was trying to stop him driving off.

Passersby were able to pin Stone down while police were being called to the scene, according to kob.com.

He was later hit with a series of charges including unlawful taking of a motor vehicle, aggravated battery and controlled substance violation.
