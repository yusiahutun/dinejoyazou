Horse Racing: John Butler in the spotlight yet again

Barney Curley’s former assistant and friend trained Low Key, one of the five horses that were part of a £2million coup on Wednesday.

Yesterday two horses trained until recently by Butler were saddled by little-known Mandy Rowland in the 5.00 at Kempton.

Pipers Piping was backed from 20-1 in to 6-4 and was unplaced while stablemate Prohibition – a beaten favourite twice last week when trained by Butler – hosed up at odds of 16-1.

Several conspiracy theorists were quick to point out the betting patterns bore the hallmark of a ‘knock-out job’, where one horse is backed to improve the odds and disguise interest in another.

Interesting times for Butler who trains Blackthorn Stick (5.30) and Misleading Promise (6.00) at Wolverhampton today.
