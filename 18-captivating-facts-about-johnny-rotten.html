Johnny Rotten was born as John Lydon on January 31, 1956, in London, England.

Johnny Rotten, also known as John Lydon, was born in London, England on January 31, His birth name was John Joseph Lydon, but he adopted the stage name Johnny Rotten during his time as the lead vocalist of the punk rock band the Sex Pistols.

He is best known as the lead singer and lyricist of the punk rock band Sex Pistols.

Johnny Rotten gained international fame as the lead singer and primary lyricist of the influential punk rock band, Sex Pistols. The band was known for their rebellious and provocative music, which challenged societal norms and gave a voice to disaffected youth.

The Sex Pistols’ debut album “Never Mind the Bollocks, Here’s the Sex Pistols” is considered a punk rock masterpiece.

Released in 1977, “Never Mind the Bollocks, Here’s the Sex Pistols” is widely regarded as one of the most important albums in punk rock history. It featured iconic tracks such as “Anarchy in the U.K.” and “God Save the Queen” that captured the spirit of the punk movement.

Johnny Rotten rose to fame as the lead vocalist and lyricist of the influential punk rock band, the Sex Pistols. The band’s provocative lyrics and rebellious energy quickly made them one of the most talked-about bands of the 1970s.
