Amy Corbett Married, Partner, Salary At LEGO Group, Net Worth

Is Amy Corbett married? Who is her partner? What is her current relationship status?

These are some of the questions frequently asked by viewers of LEGO Masters fans.

After two successful seasons, LEGO Masters is back for season 3 to excite the inner child of viewers on FOX. The new show premiered on 5 June 2022. Hosted by Will Arnett, the FOX competition show earned praises from the fans for the diversity and inclusivity of the contestants.

But, besides the show’s inclusive nature, fans primarily loved the contestant’s vibrant worlds made from interlocking parts and challenges the contestants overcome. The talent of the Lego creators has the examined, and the ones doing the honor are Amy Corbett and Jamie Berard. She is not only the Lego judge but an expert working at LEGO Group.

In season 2 of the show, each team wears a DOTS bracelet, from the Lego DOTS line Amy worked on and called her “baby”, to honor deceased family members. She shared her bracelet on Instagram and shared that “she had a custom bracelet for every episode of the show”.

Amy Corbett On LEGO Masters

Amy Corbett also talked to Decider. She wanted viewers to prepare to “see LEGO in totally brand new ways that you’ve never seen before”. Challenges will see contestants dip a toe into the fashion world and even build LEGO parade floats. “It always amazes us what people manage to do with LEGO bricks,” she shared with The Brothers Brick.

She had previously worked for The Lego Movie 2 as a part of the concept team.

Speaking to OprahDaily, Amy shared, “It’s been an absolute whirlwind”. She’s been delighted by both the LEGO Masters team and the talented builders who compete in the show. Watching season one, “you also get to see what you look like, what funny little quirks you have,” she said, but making season 2 felt a bit more relaxing. “We just had fun the second time around, because we kind of knew what we were doing,” Amy told OprahDaily.

A few of her IG followers asked questions and one of them was, “Was being on a TV show part of your life plan?” Amy answered, “I always knew I wanted to be a designer and create but never in my wildest dreams did I think I’d be making toys and TV with lego”.

She added, “I think all of my most awesome adventures have happened outside of the plan. Just hard work and being brave enough to take opportunities as they come”.

Amy is also an on-and-off screen friend with fellow judge Jamie.

Amy completed her high school at Eastwood High School in 2006. She attended Glasgow School of Art she wanted a creative role which led her to study Product Design Engineering. She earned her degree in 2012.

The Glasgow native started her career in Uganda where she worked for four months as a design engineer in Engineers Without Borders UK. After graduating college, she enrolled at Buggage Ltd as a director | designer. In 2013, she joined The LEGO Group as a senior designer.

In the IG Q&A, Amy answered a question that she had thought about when she realized LEGO was her calling. However, the day she set her foot in the LEGO offices, she knew it was her calling for sure.

“Working for LEGO was number one on the list,” Amy recounted to OprahDaily. Two weeks later, a job opened up, “and the rest was destiny”.

Amy is currently based at LEGO headquarters in Denmark. Starting from 2017 as design manager. Moreover, she was promoted to Senior Design Manager in August 2019, her current role.

2 August 2021, Amy made a post celebrating her 8th year working in LEGO Group. She captioned, “When I stepped off that plane in cloudy Denmark, little did I know the incredible adventures that were going to come my way… I mean, I still can’t get over the fact I had this LEGO Friends character based on me”.

She added, “But seriously…it has been the BEST 8 years of my life, filled with incredible people, wild experiences, and oh so much fun&laughter! I feel so wonderfully lucky and my heart is full”.

Amy Corbett Family

Amy Corbett has featured both of her parents on her IG. On Mother’s Day post-2020, she captioned, “Today is Mother’s Day in the UK, so sending lots of love to the fabulous, creative, and inspiring lady I get to call my Mom. And to all you amazing moms out there. You rock!”

She concluded, “She was due to come to visit me next week, but with everything happening right now, we will have to stick to video chat instead. Looking forward to brighter times ahead, when I can see her for a (very) big hug!”. She often received compliments on Instagram for looking like her mother.

Likewise, on Father’s Day 2020, Amy posted, “Happy Father’s Day to all you fabulous Dads out there! Special shout out to mine. The LEGO-loving, gin pouring, chef extraordinaire. Always there to give a few wise words and a HUGE hug!!! Counting down the days until I can enjoy a real-life one, a home-cooked meal, and a perfectly poured gin. Until then, Zoom will have to do. Love you:”.

Amy also has a brother named William Corbett. He studied Business & Management at the University of Glasgow and graduated in 2015. He has been working as a Content Manager at Sunshine Communications and Brand Officer at Worldwide Cancer Research.
