What nationality is Barry Keoghan?

Despite speculation that he may have had Chinese or Korean roots, Barry Keoghan is a proudly Irish actor.

Barry Keoghan has starred in a string of hit films

If you thought Keoghan looked familiar, that’s because he probably is.

The Dublin born actor, 31, has starred in several films and TV shows including King of the Travellers, The Killing of a Sacred Deer, Christopher Nolan’s Dunkirk, Chernobyl, Marvel’s Eternals, DC’s The Batman and Top Boy. He’s showing no signs of slowing down either, with upcoming TV shows Masters of the Air on Apple TV alongside Austin Butler and Irish thriller Bring Them Down already in the can.

Per Mamamia, he’s also won a BAFTA and received nominations for a Golden Globe and Academy Award for the 2022 film Banshees of Inisherin.

His childhood wasn’t easy

Keoghan faced huge challenges in his childhood. According to his 2018 interview on The Late Late Show, he grew up in Ireland during a heroin epidemic. “The drugs hit the area, and it affected all the families and she was one of them that got caught,” he said, explaining how his mother died from an overdose.

“I was living with my nanny at that stage … I was 12. [My mother] was very young. She was 30, 31 … I have great memories of her and I’m very proud of her.” He also talked about how he was then taken into foster care, living with 13 different families in total.
