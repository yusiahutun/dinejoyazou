Best female rappers of all time: Top 10 rap artists who shaped hip-hop

Rapping requires plenty of practice hours and the ability to choose words carefully. Unlike the past, where rappers were associated with defiance of moral authority, rapping is a widely accepted form of art now. Most young people enjoy rap music and adore the rappers they consider the best in the industry. Who are the best female rappers?

Female rappers are talented, with some being better on the mic than their male counterparts. These female rappers are diverse, with some dropping straight bars on 90s beats while others using mostly today's trap and Afro beats.

Best female rappers

Most well-known names in hip hop are mainly male rappers, but popularity does not indicate that the talent disparity is big. Some of the top women in hip-hop are pretty good, and they only need more support. This list features some of the best female music artists:

1. Nicki Minaj

Nicki is a Trinidadian-born singer, songwriter, and television personality. She rocketed to fame with tracks like Super Bass, Starships, and later Anaconda. She is the first female solo artist to have seven singles simultaneously on the Billboard 100 chart.

2. Cardi B

Her commercial debut single, Bodak Yellow, surged to the top of the music charts in 2017. On April 6, 2018, she unveiled her long-awaited debut album, Invasion of Privacy. Her star has continued to shine with the many awards under her name.

3. Missy Elliott

Melissa Arnette Elliott, better known as Missy Elliott, is not a new name in the rap scene. She is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer. Her first album, Supa Dupa Fly (1997), went platinum and was nominated for a Grammy Award. Her follow-up, Da Real World (1999), spent almost a year on the Billboard rhythm-and-blues chart.

4. Queen Latifah

Queen Latifah is an old-school female rapper. She is known for her acting chops as she is her lyrical pen. Back in the day, she rapped circles around the average MC. The musician signed with Tommy Boy Records in 1989 and released All Hail the Queen that same year.

5. Remy Ma

Remy was initially discovered by Big Pun and came to prominence as a member of Fat Joe's Terror Squad crew. She released her first solo album; There's Something About Remy: Based on a True Story, in 2006. She is one of only six female rappers to top the Billboard Hot 100 chart, which she did with The Terror Squad.

6. MC Lyte

Lana Michele Moorer, popularly known as MC Lyte, is a godmother in the rap scene. She got the first gold certification by a female solo rap artist for her hit, Ruffneck, and is widely cited as an influence on stars like Missy Elliott and Lil Kim. But, long before female MCs were prevalent, she was coming for anyone willing to challenge her.

7. Trina

Katrina Laverne Taylor is an American rapper, songwriter, television personality, and actress. She rose to prominence in 1998, with her appearance on the Trick Daddy single Nann Nigga. She has been in the spotlight both for her rapping and her appearances in the tabloids.

8. Da Brat

Shawntae Harris, better known by her stage name Da Brat, is an American rapper and actress. She began her career in 1992 by signing with So So Def Records. Her debut album Funkdafied sold one million copies, making her the first female solo rap act to receive a platinum certification after Salt-N-Pepa. She is one of the female rap artists doing big in the industry.

9. Salt-N-Pepa

Salt, Pepa, and DJ Spinderella came together in 1985 and were considered the most popular female hip-hop artists of all time. Their fourth album, Fundamental, gave the trio their first Grammy Award on account of the single Not Your Issue to worry about.
